üîß Technical Curiosity

What tech stack did you use?

SimulateAI is built with modern web technologies: vanilla JavaScript for the frontend, Firebase for backend services, Chart.js for radar visualizations, and Vite for development tooling. We prioritize performance, accessibility, and cross-platform compatibility.

Is this built as a PWA or native app?

SimulateAI is a Progressive Web App (PWA) that works seamlessly across all devices. You can install it on mobile devices for offline access to scenarios and a native app-like experience, while maintaining full cross-platform compatibility.

How did you manage scenario branching and metadata?

We use a JSON-based scenario engine with decision trees and weighted ethical dimensions. Each choice maps to our 8-dimensional ethical framework (Fairness, Sustainability, Autonomy, etc.) and influences the final radar chart visualization through algorithmic scoring.

How scalable is the platform?

SimulateAI is built on Firebase, which auto-scales to handle millions of users. We use efficient caching, lazy loading, and optimized database queries to ensure fast performance even with large classroom deployments and concurrent usage.

Do you use Firebase?

Yes! We leverage Firebase Authentication for secure login, Firestore for real-time data, Firebase Storage for curriculum resources, and Firebase Analytics for educational insights. This ensures reliability, security, and FERPA compliance.

Is the source code open?

SimulateAI is currently proprietary, but we're exploring open-source components for educational scenarios and ethical frameworks. We believe in transparency and may open-source the core scenario engine in the future to benefit the broader educational community.

How do you handle user data and consent?

We're FERPA and COPPA compliant with strict data protection. Student data is encrypted, never sold, and used only for educational purposes. Parents and educators maintain full control with easy data export/deletion. See our Privacy Notice for details.

üß† Philosophical & Research Driven

What ethical frameworks does SimulateAI use?

We integrate four major ethical traditions: Virtue Ethics (Aristotle), Utilitarianism (Mill), Deontological Ethics (Kant), and Care Ethics (Gilligan). Our 8-dimensional framework maps to these traditions while remaining accessible to K-12 students. See our Ethics Guide for details.

How is this different from a regular ethics quiz?

Unlike quizzes with "right" answers, SimulateAI presents complex scenarios where multiple ethical choices are valid. Students explore consequences, consider stakeholder perspectives, and develop reasoning skills rather than memorizing rules. The focus is on ethical thinking, not ethical answers.

Do you collect data for research?

Only with explicit consent and IRB approval. We partner with universities to study ethical reasoning development, but all research is anonymous, voluntary, and focused on improving AI ethics education. Students and educators can opt out at any time.

Can we analyze trends across user decisions?

Educators can view aggregated, anonymous classroom trends to understand common ethical reasoning patterns. Individual student data remains private. We're developing research dashboards for districts interested in understanding ethical development across their programs.

üöÄ Origin Story & Roadmap

How did this project start?

SimulateAI began as a research project exploring how young people reason about AI ethics. We realized traditional ethics education wasn't preparing students for AI-driven futures, so we built an interactive platform that makes complex ethical thinking accessible and engaging.

What challenges did you face building it?

Balancing academic rigor with student engagement was our biggest challenge. We needed scenarios complex enough to prompt genuine ethical reasoning but accessible to diverse learners. Countless iterations with educators and students helped us find that sweet spot.

What's next for SimulateAI?

2025 roadmap includes: custom scenario builder, advanced analytics dashboard, mobile app, multilingual support, and partnerships with major curricula. We're also exploring VR ethics simulations and AI-generated discussion prompts for personalized learning.

Can I donate or contribute scenarios?

Yes! We welcome donations through our support page and collaborate with educators on scenario development. Email research@simulateai.io to discuss contributing your ethical dilemmas or real-world case studies.

The radar chart won't load

First, refresh the page. If the issue persists, check that cdn.jsdelivr.net is not blocked by your district firewall (Chart.js loads from that CDN). Whitelisting the domain usually resolves the problem. Contact IT if you need help with firewall settings.

Students can't access assigned scenarios

Verify that students are logged in with the correct accounts linked to your classroom. Check that the assignment is published and hasn't expired. You can force-refresh class data from Educator Tools ‚Üí Dashboard ‚Üí Sync Classroom.
